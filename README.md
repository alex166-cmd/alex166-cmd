## ⚡ Energy Demand Forecasting & KPI Monitoring

**Stata • Time Series Analysis • SARIMA**

A monitoring and evaluation project simulating operational reporting in Malawi's electricity sector. The project combines KPI monitoring with time-series forecasting to support planning and decision-making.

### Dataset

* Monthly electricity sector data
* January 2018 – December 2024
* 84 observations
* 14 operational variables

### KPIs Monitored

* Generation Adequacy Ratio
* Capacity Utilization
* Energy Access Gap
* Hydro Dependency Ratio
* Reliability Index
* Annual Demand Growth

### Analytical Methods

* Data validation and consistency checks
* Exploratory Data Analysis
* Augmented Dickey-Fuller test
* ACF and PACF diagnostics
* ARIMA and SARIMA modelling
* Forecast accuracy evaluation using MAE, RMSE and MAPE

### Key Findings

* Demand consistently exceeded generation capacity.
* Seasonal demand peaks occurred during the dry season.
* SARIMA achieved a forecasting accuracy below 5% MAPE.
* Reliability declined from 93.3% to 91.9% over the study period.
* Forecasts indicated widening supply deficits without additional generation capacity.

### Tools Used

* Stata 17
* Time Series Forecasting
* Statistical Reporting
* Monitoring & Evaluation

🔗 Repository: **energy-forecasting-malawi**

## 🏥 Forecasting Non-Communicable Diseases at QECH

**R • ARIMA • Applied Statistics**

Undergraduate thesis project conducted at Queen Elizabeth Central Hospital (QECH), Malawi, aimed at forecasting future prevalence of non-communicable diseases to support healthcare planning.

### Research Objective

To determine whether historical admission data can be used to forecast future disease burdens and support resource allocation.

### Diseases Studied

* Hypertension
* Diabetes Mellitus
* Cardiovascular Disease

### Methodology

* Time-series decomposition
* Augmented Dickey-Fuller test
* ARIMA model selection
* Forecasting
* Accuracy evaluation using RMSE, MAE and MAPE

### Key Findings

* Hypertension showed the strongest growth trend.
* Diabetes admissions exhibited seasonal patterns.
* Cardiovascular disease demonstrated the highest volatility.
* Forecasts suggested increasing future demand for healthcare resources.

### Tools Used

* R
* Forecast Package
* ggplot2
* LaTeX
